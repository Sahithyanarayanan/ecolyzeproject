E. coli & Gene Analysis

Learn the biological background and data sources behind our AI-powered prediction system.

What is E.coli?
These bacteria are naturally found in the human intestinal tract and are an essential part of the gut microbiome.
The majority of these bacterial strains are harmless and even play a beneficial role in digestion and maintaining overall gut health.
However, certain strains can become harmful and may lead to serious infections, especially under specific conditions.
Computational Data Collection
The dataset used in this project is sourced from the NCBI (National Center for Biotechnology Information) database, which provides reliable and well-curated biological data.
The genomic data was downloaded in FASTA (.fna) format, a standard file format used for representing nucleotide sequences.
This data is utilized for training and testing the AI model to ensure accurate analysis and prediction of biological patterns.
Real-Time Data Collection
DNA is carefully extracted from biological samples using standardized laboratory procedures to ensure purity and accuracy.
The extracted DNA is amplified using PCR tools and laboratory techniques to generate sufficient quantities for sequencing and further analysis.
Finally, the amplified DNA is sequenced and stored in FASTA format, which allows easy access and processing for computational analysis.
Importance of Sequence Analysis
The system identifies specific gene patterns associated with pathogenic organisms, helping to distinguish harmful sequences from non-harmful ones.
It supports early and accurate disease prediction by analyzing genetic data and detecting potential risk factors.
The processed data enables efficient AI-based classification, allowing the model to categorize and interpret genetic sequences effectively.
